Justifying the Internet

September 7, 2025

The internet is not a place for communication.

We talk, shitpost, shop, and consume content, but the internet as originally intended (in my opinion) is a power redistribution platform.

Exhibit A: Contractor at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee proposes hypertext, a concept that allows for the linking of documents and the creation of an interconnected web of information so scientists can stop depending on traditional methods to update each other. Faster, freer information access = less reliance on gatekeepers, potential saboteurs and more power to the individual.

Exhibit B: Satoshi Nakamoto, anonymous creator of Bitcoin, introduces a decentralized digital currency that allows for peer-to-peer (bankless) transactions without the need for intermediaries. Exhibit C: The rise of social media platforms has enabled individuals to share their thoughts and ideas without the need for traditional media gatekeepers. This has led to a more diverse range of voices being heard, including that of degenerates, bad faith agents and attention needy people.

How I justify the internet will mostly surround around Exhibit C.
